# Nebraska Board of Medicine and Surgery — 12-Month Board Summary

**Period:** 2026-01-09 to 2026-06-12
**Meetings analyzed:** 7

Over the first half of 2026, the Nebraska Board of Medicine and Surgery's agenda was dominated by pending state legislation, several scope-of-practice opinions, and routine licensure and disciplinary business. The Board opened the year by electing new officers — Wesley Zeger, DO as Chairperson, Mark Goodman, MD as Vice-Chairperson, and Jeff Howorth as Secretary — and reaffirmed its public-notice posting practices ([2026-01-09](/board/NE/NE_MD#2026-01-09)).

The most sustained storyline was LB1212, a bill to license internationally trained physicians under the Uniform Credentialing Act. The Board first flagged the bill as one to watch ([2026-01-09](/board/NE/NE_MD#2026-01-09)), then convened an emergency conference-call meeting to vote formal opposition and direct Dr. Hedrick to draft a letter of opposition for delivery as oral testimony at the legislative hearing ([2026-02-09](/board/NE/NE_MD#2026-02-09)). At its next regular meeting, the Board reviewed and approved Hedrick's testimony, which raised concerns about verifying home-country board certification, requiring recent clinical practice, and revising "limited/restricted" license terminology to "provisional license" ([2026-02-13](/board/NE/NE_MD#2026-02-13)). The Board continued to track the bill's amendments through the spring ([2026-03-13](/board/NE/NE_MD#2026-03-13)) and, after LB1212 passed, discussed the resulting need to reopen its regulations for updates ([2026-06-12](/board/NE/NE_MD#2026-06-12)).

Scope-of-practice questions recurred throughout the period. The Board opined that a Surgical First Assistant may not close a surgical site without a physician physically present, citing the statutory "personal supervision" requirement ([2026-04-10](/board/NE/NE_MD#2026-04-10)), and reaffirmed that opinion in response to a follow-up letter two months later ([2026-06-12](/board/NE/NE_MD#2026-06-12)). The Board also weighed in on low-level laser (red light) therapy as constituting the practice of medicine and surgery, on continuing-education gaps for pediatric Botox injections, and on the boundaries of NP/PA pain-management practice ([2026-05-08](/board/NE/NE_MD#2026-05-08)), and later opined that ketamine use in mental-health therapy settings requires supervision by appropriate medical providers ([2026-06-12](/board/NE/NE_MD#2026-06-12)).

Other notable items included a vendor presentation on the EBAS ethics/boundaries assessment tool for licensees under discipline ([2026-03-13](/board/NE/NE_MD#2026-03-13)); the Board's licensure recommendations on individual physician applications, including a denial based on prior out-of-state discipline and a probationary reinstatement ([2026-04-10](/board/NE/NE_MD#2026-04-10)); and a hearing in which the Board affirmed a prior denial of license-restriction removal for a named physician ([2026-06-12](/board/NE/NE_MD#2026-06-12)). Dr. Hedrick was designated the Board's voting delegate to the FSMB Annual Meeting in Baltimore ([2026-03-13](/board/NE/NE_MD#2026-03-13)), and Board members reported back on FSMB sessions, including one on AI's role in regulating medicine and surgery ([2026-05-08](/board/NE/NE_MD#2026-05-08)).

=== MEETING: 2026-06-12 ===
topics: [scope-of-practice, controlled-substances, disciplinary, legislation, rulemaking]

The Board adopted the agenda and approved the May 8, 2026 minutes. Eric Sutton (NMA) provided legislative updates, including LB527 (Medicaid Access and Quality Act) and a pending 407 Credentialing Review for Certified Nurse Midwives. The Board noted that LB1212, licensing internationally trained physicians, had passed and that its regulations would need to be reopened for updates. There were no applications for review. In discussion items, the Board reaffirmed its prior opinion that closure of a surgical site requires the personal supervision (physical presence) of a physician, citing Nebraska Statutes 38-3508 and 38-3511, in response to a follow-up letter regarding its April 10, 2026 opinion. The Board also opined that ketamine use by a patient in therapy provided by a Licensed Mental Health Practitioner needs to be supervised by appropriate medical providers. In a hearing, the Board voted to affirm its prior denial of removal of a license restriction for Daniel Martin Wik, MD, citing insufficient evidence to support removal. The next meeting was set for August 14, 2026.

=== MEETING: 2026-05-08 ===
topics: [scope-of-practice, CME, legislation, AI]

Chairperson Zeger called the meeting to order. The Board approved the agenda and the April 10, 2026 minutes. Amy Reynoldson and Eric Sutton (NMA) discussed the Rural Health Care Transformation Program and recent legislative impacts (LB527, LB77). No regulations updates were reported. An email ballot approving the Board's May 2026 newsletter draft was noted as approved. In discussion, the Board opined that low-level laser (red light) therapy constitutes the practice of medicine and surgery since it penetrates deeper than the dermis and requires appropriate scope, education, training, and informed consent. The Board addressed continuing-education gaps for therapeutic Botox injections for pediatric spasticity and stated PAs may practice pain management within their scope and their supervising physician's scope, while NP scope questions were referred to the Board of Nursing. The Board acknowledged an AOA letter of concern on patient safety. Dr. Hedrick and Dr. Blake reported on the FSMB Annual Meeting in Baltimore (April 30–May 2, 2026), including a session on AI's role and regulation in medicine and surgery; the 2027 FSMB meeting will be in Oklahoma City.

=== MEETING: 2026-04-10 ===
topics: [licensing, disciplinary, scope-of-practice, legislation]

Chairperson Zeger called the meeting to order with full attendance. The Board removed three applications from the consent agenda for individual review. NMA representatives Paul Henderson and Eric Sutton briefed the Board on several bills, including LB1212 (internationally trained physician licensure, expected to pass), LB955, LB958, LB912, LB929, and LB933 (which did not pass). The Board approved the March 13, 2026 minutes. In application review, the Board recommended denial of Wazir Ali, MD's initial license application due to prior disciplinary action in another state; recommended reinstatement of Joshua T. Evans, MD's license subject to three years' probation, including a one-year reentry license, due to a lapse in clinical practice and prior discipline; and recommended approval of a Temporary Visiting Faculty Permit for Dariia Simchuk, MD. The Board acknowledged receipt of Pharmaceutical Care Agreements from January and March 2026, and opined that a Surgical First Assistant may not close the skin without a physician's personal supervision in the operating room. The Board also noted regulations will need revision following NCBAHM's name change from NCCAOM.

=== MEETING: 2026-03-13 ===
topics: [legislation, disciplinary, rulemaking]

Vice-Chairperson Goodman called the meeting to order; Chairperson Zeger was absent. The Board postponed a scheduled hearing to a future meeting. NMA representatives Amy Reynoldson, Paul Henderson, and Eric Sutton updated the Board on LB1212 (a hearing was held February 11, 2026, and Amendment AM2477 was filed) and LB933, plus noted a stalled midwife bill and a possible 407 Credentialing Review for athletic trainer operating-room privileges. The Board approved the February 9, 2026 emergency-meeting minutes and the February 13, 2026 minutes (with a correction). Bradley Guye of EBAS presented on the company's ethics/boundaries assessment services for disciplinary processes, describing its testing process, assessment types, and $400-per-assessment cost borne by the licensee; the Board said it would highly consider using EBAS in future disciplinary matters. There were no applications for review. The Board formally nominated Dr. Hedrick as its voting delegate to the FSMB Annual Conference in Baltimore, MD (April 30–May 2, 2026). The next meeting was set for April 10, 2026.

=== MEETING: 2026-02-13 ===
topics: [legislation, licensing, disciplinary]

Chairperson Zeger called the meeting to order. The Board postponed a scheduled hearing to the March 13, 2026 meeting and removed two applications from the consent agenda. === MEETING: 2026-02-09 ===
topics: [legislation]

The Board held an emergency conference-call meeting called to order by Chairperson Zeger to discuss LB1212, the bill to license internationally trained physicians, ahead of its February 11, 2026 legislative hearing. NMA representatives Amy Reynoldson and Paul Henderson briefed the Board on the bill. After discussion of whether to take a position of opposition, neutrality, or support, the Board voted to oppose LB1212 as currently written and directed Dr. Hedrick to draft a letter of opposition for the Board's approval. The Board further voted to have Dr. Hedrick deliver the approved letter as oral testimony at the February 11, 2026 legislative hearing. No other business was conducted at this brief, single-topic emergency session.
