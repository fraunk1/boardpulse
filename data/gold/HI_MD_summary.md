# Hawaii Medical Board — 12-Month Board Summary

**Period:** 2025-08-01 to 2026-02-12
**Meetings analyzed:** 5

Over the period reviewed, the Hawaii Medical Board's agendas were dominated by routine licensure processing and disciplinary resolution, alongside a recurring focus on the Interstate Medical Licensure Compact (IMLC) and the 2026 legislative session. On disciplinary matters, the Board approved settlement agreements and final orders in numerous physician cases, including Aman K. Patel, D.O. ([2025-08-01](/board/HI/HI_MD#2025-08-01), [2025-12-11](/board/HI/HI_MD#2025-12-11)), and approved a summary suspension of Paul Dillaway's license pending a Wyoming investigatory process ([2025-08-01](/board/HI/HI_MD#2025-08-01)); it later accepted Dillaway's voluntary license relinquishment ([2025-12-11](/board/HI/HI_MD#2025-12-11)). Additional final orders and settlements were approved for David Nathaniel Smith, M.D., Alexander D. Lee, M.D., and Bernard K. Chun, M.D. ([2025-11-13](/board/HI/HI_MD#2025-11-13)), and for Sinikka Liisa Green, M.D. and Stephen A. Feig, D.O. ([2025-12-11](/board/HI/HI_MD#2025-12-11)).

The IMLC was a recurring theme. The Board delegated authority to the Executive Officer to sign IMLC-related final orders to expedite licensure ([2025-12-11](/board/HI/HI_MD#2025-12-11)) and rescinded letters of qualification for three physicians following notice from the Compact ([2025-12-11](/board/HI/HI_MD#2025-12-11)). Dr. Jaffe reported to the Board on IMLC statistics following the Compact's annual meeting in Denver, noting the Compact spans 44 states and has produced over 49,000 licenses, but that Hawaii cannot yet serve as a Principal State of Licensure because the FBI has not approved the state to conduct required background checks ([2025-12-11](/board/HI/HI_MD#2025-12-11)).

On legislation and national engagement, the Board confirmed its 2026 legislative liaisons, reviewed a color-coded list of 2026 bills, reaffirmed that all individuals treating Hawaii patients — regardless of telehealth or in-person modality — must hold a Hawaii license, and delegated liaisons to provide testimony between meetings ([2026-02-12](/board/HI/HI_MD#2026-02-12)). The Board also discussed comments on three draft FSMB reports (physician collective bargaining, prescribing/dispensing trends, and oversight of clinical decision-making) and selected its delegation for the FSMB 2026 Annual Meeting in Baltimore, with Chair Takanishi serving as Voting Delegate amid his candidacy for the FSMB Board of Directors ([2026-02-12](/board/HI/HI_MD#2026-02-12)); the Board had earlier endorsed his nomination ([2025-12-11](/board/HI/HI_MD#2025-12-11)). The Board welcomed new member Dr. Robert Wotring ([2026-02-12](/board/HI/HI_MD#2026-02-12)) and granted a CME extension to one licensee ([2026-02-12](/board/HI/HI_MD#2026-02-12)). Routine business also included licensure applications, ratification lists, and reappointments to the EMS Personnel Advisory Committee ([2025-11-13](/board/HI/HI_MD#2025-11-13)).

=== MEETING: 2026-02-12 ===
topics: [IMLC, licensing, CME, legislation, telehealth]

The Board welcomed new member Dr. Robert Wotring and approved the January 15, 2026 minutes. Following executive session, the Board approved several physician applications, including those of Jaclyn Tolentino, D.O., Jim Seraj, M.D., Timothy R. Taylor, M.D., and Robert Matthew Bernstein, M.D.; deferred Nessa Seangmany, D.O.'s application pending a military discharge record and substance use evaluation; and approved Wayne Tran, M.D.'s application subject to quarterly California Medical Board Probationary Unit reports and, if he relocates to Hawaii, a Hawaii Physicians Health Program arrangement. The Board granted Miles Wayne Howard, M.D. a three-month CME extension. For the 2026 legislative session, the Board confirmed Dr. Jaffe as an additional legislative liaison, reviewed a color-coded bill tracking list, reaffirmed that anyone treating Hawaii patients via telehealth or in-person must be licensed in the state, and delegated liaisons to provide testimony between meetings. Members discussed three draft FSMB reports and selected delegates for FSMB's 2026 Annual Meeting in Baltimore, with Chair Takanishi as Voting Delegate, Vice Chair Belcher as Public Member Scholarship recipient, and EO Kor as Senior Staff Scholarship recipient. The Board also flagged a scam phone campaign targeting licensees.

=== MEETING: 2025-12-11 ===
topics: [IMLC, licensing, disciplinary, legislation]

With Vice Chair Belcher presiding, the Board approved a second final order revoking Aman K. Patel, D.O.'s restricted license, a settlement agreement for Sinikka Liisa Green, M.D., and a final order against Stephen A. Feig, D.O. It approved licensure applications for Elias Elias, M.D., Kamshad Raiszadeh, M.D., Frank Hiroshi Harris, M.D., and Alan Brewer, M.D., approved Diana Gliga, M.D.'s restoration with conditional reinstatement, and deferred Kenneth Carl Russ, M.D. The Board delegated IMLC final-order signing authority to the Executive Officer, accepted Paul Dillaway's voluntary license relinquishment, and rescinded IMLC-based licenses for Dheeraj Makkar, M.D., Sue Xuan Poon, M.D., and Paul E. Cox, M.D. Dr. Jaffe reported on the IMLC annual meeting in Denver, noting the Compact covers 44 states with over 49,000 licenses issued, 54% of recipients serving rural/underserved areas, and 51% licensed within seven days; he explained Hawaii cannot yet become a Principal State of Licensure because the FBI has denied the state background-check authority, a gap DAG Wong said staff are addressing through the Hawaii Criminal Justice Data Center. The Board also endorsed Chair Takanishi's FSMB Board of Directors nomination and discussed proposed Physician Assistant Compact legislation.
