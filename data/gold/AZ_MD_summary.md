# Arizona Medical Board — 12-Month Board Summary

**Period:** 2025-07-09 to 2026-07-21
**Meetings analyzed:** 26

Over the past year, the Arizona Medical Board's work centered on scope-of-practice rulemaking, licensure legislation, board leadership transitions, and a steady docket of disciplinary matters. A recurring thread was whether medical assistants may administer anesthetic injections: the Administrative Joint Legislation and Rules Committee (JLRC) fielded physician and stakeholder input (including from the Arizona Society of Anesthesiologists and dermatology groups) across multiple sessions ([2025-08-26](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2025-08-26), [2025-09-22](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2025-09-22), [2025-10-30](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2025-10-30), [2026-02-12](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2026-02-12)), culminating in the Board's approval of MA FAQ revisions capping dosage at 3 ccs per injection up to 300 mg ([2026-03-04](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2026-03-04)). Related rulemaking on Article 7 (Office-Based Surgery) and Article 2 (Licensure) advanced through the Governor's Regulatory Review Council over the year ([2025-07-31](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2025-07-31), [2025-09-22](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2025-09-22), [2025-10-30](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2025-10-30), [2026-02-12](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2026-02-12)), with a public-comment rulemaking notice issued for July 2026 ([2026-07-21](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2026-07-21)).

Legislatively, the Board repeatedly opposed bills creating alternative licensure pathways for internationally trained physicians without ECFMG certification (HB 2435), citing concerns about English-language competency and supervision burdens in rural areas ([2026-02-04](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2026-02-04), [2026-02-12](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2026-02-12), [2026-03-04](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2026-03-04)). The Board also opposed HB 2660, which would have restricted the Board's ability to act on pending criminal charges, and SB 1124, concerning notice requirements for board-ordered evaluations ([2026-02-04](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2026-02-04)).

Leadership changed hands during the year: Raquel Rivera was selected as Executive Director following a multi-meeting interview process by the ED Selection and Retention Committee ([2025-07-09](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2025-07-09), [2025-07-28](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2025-07-28)), and the Committee later voted to cease her Performance Incentive Pay pending revised metrics, following Sunset Audit recommendations ([2026-02-23](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2026-02-23)). The Board also lost long-serving members Dr. Lois Krahn and Jessyca Leach ([2025-12-03](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2025-12-03), [2025-11-05](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2025-11-05)) amid ongoing struggles to fill Senate-confirmed seats ([2025-07-09](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2025-07-09)).

Disciplinary highlights included the summary suspension of Dr. Paul Aupperle for repeated positive methamphetamine tests in violation of a prior Board order ([2026-01-09](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2026-01-09)); a contested summary-suspension vote involving Dr. Josef Sipos over boundary violations, which failed before the Board instead offered an Interim Consent Agreement ([2025-10-20](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2025-10-20)); and the summary suspension of Dr. Richard Warrington for an unaddressed impairing medical condition ([2025-09-17](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2025-09-17)). The Board also worked through a Letter of Reprimand and Probation for Dr. Heramb Singh over inadequate documentation following a serious CESI complication ([2025-08-06](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2025-08-06)).

Operationally, the Board relocated to Google Meets for teleconferencing, reported IMLC revenue of $839,750 for FY26 ([2025-12-03](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2025-12-03)), and discussed AI's potential role in regulatory communication and legislative tracking through FSMB's Administrators in Medicine work ([2025-12-03](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2025-12-03)). A June 2025 flood at the Board's 1740 W. Adams building caused significant equipment damage and delayed the return to in-person meetings ([2025-12-03](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2025-12-03), [2026-04-01](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2026-04-01), [2026-05-06](/board/AZ/AZ_MD#2026-05-06)).
