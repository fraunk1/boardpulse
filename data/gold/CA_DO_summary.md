# Osteopathic Medical Board of California — 12-Month Board Summary

**Period:** 2025-08-14 to 2026-04-23
**Meetings analyzed:** 4

Over the past year the Osteopathic Medical Board of California (OMBC) has been dominated by two long-running rulemaking packages, a steady rise in enforcement and licensing volume, and a board membership and leadership transition. The Board's central regulatory priority has been the "Retired License, Petitions and Fees" package, which raises fees to statutory maximums, creates a retired license status, and adds petition/adjudication fees to fund the process. Staff trimmed the package to focus solely on fees after board members flagged application-language additions as overly ambitious ([2025-11-13](/board/CA/CA_DO#2025-11-13)); the Board approved the revised regulatory text at that meeting, and by April 2026 the package (over 100 pages) had cleared Regulations Counsel and the DCA Budget Office and was submitted to the Acting DCA Director for review ([2026-04-23](/board/CA/CA_DO#2026-04-23)). A separate, long-delayed Disciplinary Guidelines rulemaking — the Board's first update since 1996 — remained in early drafting throughout the year, with staff repeatedly pushing back its expected Board review date, most recently to as early as the May 2026 meeting ([2026-01-22](/board/CA/CA_DO#2026-01-22)).

Fiscal pressure was a recurring theme: the Board has not raised fees since 1994, is projected to fall to about one month of reserve by 2027, and saw its reserve cushion shrink from roughly 11.9 months in August 2025 to about 7.4 months by January 2026 ([2025-08-14](/board/CA/CA_DO#2025-08-14), [2026-01-22](/board/CA/CA_DO#2026-01-22)). Enforcement volume rose sharply across the year — complaints and arrest/conviction notices climbed from 1,011 for all of FY 2024/25 to 937 through just the first three quarters of FY 2025/26, a 22% year-over-year increase, alongside a 48% jump in pending formal investigations ([2025-08-14](/board/CA/CA_DO#2025-08-14), [2026-04-23](/board/CA/CA_DO#2026-04-23)). The Board also gained expanded administrative citation authority effective October 1, 2025, which it began using to close more minor-violation cases without formal discipline ([2025-11-13](/board/CA/CA_DO#2025-11-13)).

Leadership changes included the retirement of DCA Director Kimberly Kirchmeyer and the appointment of Christine Lally as Acting DCA Director ([2026-01-22](/board/CA/CA_DO#2026-01-22)); the departure of longtime board member Andrew Moreno after seven years and the appointment of attorney Negeen Mirreghabie ([2025-11-13](/board/CA/CA_DO#2025-11-13)); the reappointment of Board President Denise Pines ([2026-01-22](/board/CA/CA_DO#2026-01-22)); and the March 2026 appointment of neurologist Dr. Faisal Qazi to the Board ([2026-04-23](/board/CA/CA_DO#2026-04-23)). The Board also launched its first legislative "Hill Day" on January 21, 2026, to advocate for a bill protecting the "Osteopath" title designation ([2026-01-22](/board/CA/CA_DO#2026-01-22)), a bill (AB 1703) the Board went on to support in its 2026 legislative positions ([2026-04-23](/board/CA/CA_DO#2026-04-23)). The Board consistently opposed bills expanding telehealth licensure exemptions for out-of-state physicians, citing loss of jurisdiction over unlicensed practitioners ([2026-04-23](/board/CA/CA_DO#2026-04-23)), and supported AB 489 restricting deceptive AI/health-care-professional terminology ([2025-11-13](/board/CA/CA_DO#2025-11-13)).

=== MEETING: 2026-04-23 ===
topics: [AI, telehealth, scope-of-practice, disciplinary, rulemaking, workforce, licensing, legislation]

The Board met at Western University of Health Sciences in Pomona and heard a hearing on a petition for early termination of probation for Jeff Douglas Lester, D.O. Executive Director Erika Calderon reported that Dr. Faisal Qazi, a Fullerton neurologist, was appointed to the Board on March 20, 2026, and that the Board maintains 15.9 authorized positions with no vacancies. Licensing reported 17,407 licensed D.O.s and a 32% increase in applications received; enforcement reported 937 total complaints/arrests year-to-date (a 22% increase), 788 pending cases, and a 48% rise in pending formal investigations, alongside 30 active probationers. The Retired License, Petitions and Fees rulemaking package advanced to the Acting DCA Director for review after clearing Budget and Regulations Counsel review. Staff presented 2026 legislative positions, including support for AB 1703 (title protection for "Osteopath" and adding OMT to physician scope of practice) and opposition to SB 1002 (out-of-state telehealth licensure exemption expansion) and AB 2398 (alternative residency pathway), citing public-safety and enforcement-jurisdiction concerns. The agenda also listed AB 2575 and SB 903 on AI in health care and AI consent for pending legislative discussion.

=== MEETING: 2026-01-22 ===
topics: [AI, telehealth, controlled-substances, disciplinary, rulemaking, workforce, licensing, legislation]

President Denise Pines announced her reappointment by the Governor and reported on the Board's inaugural "Hill Day" (January 21, 2026), where a delegation met with legislators to build support for future D.O.-title legislation. She also noted the FSMB Office of AI Innovation continues to monitor AI regulation. DCA reported Christine Lally as Acting Director following Kimberly Kirchmeyer's retirement, a balanced 2026-27 Governor's budget, and a wildfire-related renewal fee postponement for Los Angeles-area licensees. The Board's reserve was projected to fall to about 7.4 months. Executive Director Calderon reported on statewide staff classification changes and a December 17, 2025 joint forum with other prescribing boards on controlled-substances access. The Board approved revisions to its 2024-2028 Strategic Plan by motion. Licensing reported 17,164 licensed D.O.s and a 27% increase in applications; enforcement reported 630 complaints and 12 arrest/conviction notices year-to-date, a 25% increase, and 733 pending cases. Rulemaking updates covered the Fees/License Status package (split from disciplinary-guideline provisions) and continued drafting of the Disciplinary Guidelines, not amended since 1996. Terri Thorfinnson reported that AB 489 (AI deception) and AB 460 (radiologist telemedicine) were signed into law, while AB 432 (menopause CME) and AB 742 (expedited licensure) were vetoed.

=== MEETING: 2025-11-13 ===
topics: [AI, disciplinary, rulemaking, workforce, patient-safety, controlled-substances, licensing, legislation]

The Board held a hearing on Eman Abdallah, D.O.'s petition for early termination of probation from a 2023 gross-negligence and unlicensed-practice-aiding case; the Attorney General recommended continuing probation. President Pines recognized outgoing member Andrew Moreno for seven years of service and welcomed new appointee Negeen Mirreghabie, a University of San Diego law professor, while reporting on FSMB's Dublin meeting on medical racism standards and an emerging "assistant physician" licensing pathway. Consumer Watchdog representatives criticized enforcement terminology and asked for public enforcement-committee meetings. Licensing reported 17,012 licensed D.O.s (up 17% in applications); enforcement reported 320 complaints for the quarter, a 33% increase, with AG referrals doubling. Probation Monitor Ralph Correa reported 31 active probationers. The Board approved trimmed regulatory text for the Retired License, Petitions and Fees package after discussion in which Dr. Lockman raised CME and financial-hardship concerns; Calderon noted the Board has not raised fees since 1994 and projects a one-month reserve deficit by 2027. A year-end legislative report showed support for AB 489 (AI: deceptive health-care terms, signed) and AB 260 (reproductive health, signed), and opposition to AB 876 (nurse anesthetist scope expansion, signed) and AB 460 (radiologic technologist telemedicine supervision, signed).

=== MEETING: 2025-08-14 ===
topics: [AI, telehealth, disciplinary, rulemaking, workforce, patient-safety, licensing, legislation]

Held at Touro University College of Osteopathic Medicine, the meeting included two early-termination-of-probation hearings: Steven Duane Western, D.O. (gross negligence treating a family member, inadequate recordkeeping) and James Michael Lally, D.O. (sexual harassment, discrimination, hostile-work-environment findings), both taken under submission. President Pines reported on a planned January 2026 "Hill Day," an FSMB work group on IV hydration and ketamine clinic trends, and FSMB's new Office of AI Innovation. Board members held a Q&A with Touro students on professional identity, licensure, and residency access. DCA reported the agency reorganization, with a return-to-office mandate postponed to July 1, 2026. Executive Director Calderon reported hiring a Special Investigator projected to save $150,000 annually in investigation costs, and a projected $694,000 annual revenue increase from new fees and citation authority. Enforcement reported 1,011 complaints for FY 2024/25 (a 26% increase) and 38 active probationers. The Board approved the CME/Audit and Cite-and-Fine rulemaking (effective October 1, 2025) and reconsidered fee-schedule language in the Applications, Fees and Retired License package. A bill status report showed Board support for AB 489 (AI: deceptive health-care terms) and opposition to SB 508 (telemedicine license exemption expansion) and AB 876 (nurse anesthetist scope expansion).
