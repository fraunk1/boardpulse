# Vermont Board of Medical Practice — 12-Month Board Summary

**Period:** 2025-07-09 to 2026-06-15
**Meetings analyzed:** 20

The source material provided for this period consists overwhelmingly of Vermont Emergency Medical Services (EMS) Advisory Committee and Education Council minutes, workgroup reports, and supporting EMS policy documents, rather than Vermont Board of Medical Practice meeting minutes in the traditional sense. The rollup below reflects only what is documented in that material.

Across the year, the EMS Advisory Committee (EMSAC) worked on the statutory five-year statewide EMS plan required under Act 157 of 2024, which formally designated EMS as an essential service ([2025-12-10](/board/VT/VT_MD#2025-12-10)). Early sessions focused on defining basic terms: the committee adopted a working definition of "Emergency Medical Services," amended to clarify that EMS determines the "appropriate disposition" of patients rather than a patient's disposition outright ([2025-08-06](/board/VT/VT_MD#2025-08-06)), and later refined that definition further at a June 2025 retreat ([2025-07-09](/board/VT/VT_MD#2025-07-09)). The committee also adopted four initial performance metrics — Hypoglycemia 01, Trauma 08, Safety 02, and Stroke 01 — drawn from national NEMSQA/NEMSIS standards ([2025-08-06](/board/VT/VT_MD#2025-08-06)), following earlier discussion of clinical and operational reliability measures and the CARES cardiac arrest registry ([2025-07-16](/board/VT/VT_MD#2025-07-16)).

A central and recurring theme was EMS system governance. Vermont's current model of 13 independent, statute-based EMS districts was repeatedly described as inconsistent and under-resourced, lacking enforcement authority and adequate funding ([2026-02-18](/board/VT/VT_MD#2026-02-18)). A Governance Subcommittee proposed a three-region model with a nine-member statewide EMS Board ([2025-09-03](/board/VT/VT_MD#2025-09-03), [2025-10-15](/board/VT/VT_MD#2025-10-15)), and by March 2026 the Governance Workgroup was developing a proposal to replace EMSAC with a statewide EMS Board while elevating the EMS Office to division-level status ([2026-03-18](/board/VT/VT_MD#2026-03-18)). In April 2026, the committee voted by roll call to approve a governance consent item establishing the Vermont Board of EMS (VBEMS) as a 21-member successor body to EMSAC ([2026-04-15](/board/VT/VT_MD#2026-04-15)).

Workforce and education issues also recurred: instructor accountability gaps, with a small number of Senior Instructors delivering most EMT and AEMT courses ([2025-08-20](/board/VT/VT_MD#2025-08-20)); debate over whether Vermont should remain a National Registry state ([2026-03-06](/board/VT/VT_MD#2026-03-06)); and consent items on field/clinical experience requirements, curriculum modernization, and VEFR realignment, all approved unanimously in May 2026 ([2026-05-20](/board/VT/VT_MD#2026-05-20)).

Finance was a persistent concern. An EMS system assessment found total annual system cost of approximately $98 million against roughly $53 million in insurance reimbursement ([2025-11-19](/board/VT/VT_MD#2025-11-19), [2025-10-15](/board/VT/VT_MD#2025-10-15)). The committee's statutory legislative report was the subject of a contested, recorded vote in December 2025 over a motion to delay submission, which failed 4-8-1 before the committee approved a final draft 11-0-2 ([2025-12-10](/board/VT/VT_MD#2025-12-10)).

=== MEETING: 2026-06-03 ===
topics: [workforce, rulemaking, legislation]

The EMS Advisory Committee met at the Waterbury State Office Complex, with significant technical difficulties (internet outage requiring cellular hotspot use) affecting recording quality. Workgroups reported out: Education & Workforce Development discussed degree requirements, paramedic practitioner pathways, and apprenticeship models; Healthcare Integration discussed interfacility transport categorization and EMS-mental health system integration; Medical Direction worked on foundational definitions; Operations discussed statewide resource tracking and GIS mapping; Finance discussed non-transport reimbursement and regional collaboration incentives. Consent item CI-FIN-001 (reimbursement for treatment without transport) was moved and seconded, but after discussion about Medicare BLS non-emergency base rate language, the motion and second were withdrawn and the item returned to the Finance Workgroup. The committee discussed the EMSAC 2026 final report/five-year plan structure and held an initial discussion on EMS Compact participation, referring it to the Governance Workgroup. VDH gave a brief update on Mobile Integrated Health and Rural Health Transformation Program efforts. The meeting adjourned at 3:48 PM.

=== MEETING: 2026-05-20 ===
topics: [workforce, rulemaking, licensing]

Held at Bennington Rescue Squad; the committee's editor's note states the meeting transcript was incomplete or corrupted, so discussion summaries were reconstructed from available portions. Four Education & Workforce Development consent items were approved unanimously: CI-EWD-006 (field and clinical experience requirements), CI-EWD-007 (curriculum and resources modernization), CI-EWD-008 (VEFR curriculum realignment), and CI-EWD-009 (assigning workforce evaluation/planning responsibility). CI-HIN-002 (interfacility transport endorsement requirement) passed by roll call vote. CI-HIN-004 (requiring IFT agreements between healthcare facilities and EMS agencies) also passed by roll call. Five Operations consent items (CI-OPS-001 through CI-OPS-005), covering statewide resource tracking, service interruption response, a statewide operational objective, a statewide operational plan, and mutual aid definitions, all passed unanimously. === MEETING: 2026-04-15 ===
topics: [rulemaking, workforce, legislation]

The Education Council and Advisory Committee met at the Waterbury State Office Complex. Minutes from the March 18 meeting were approved unanimously. The chair emphasized that the meeting's purpose was to vote on workgroup-developed consent items rather than re-litigate workgroup discussions. The committee took up Governance Consent Item #1 (Revision A), establishing the Vermont Board of EMS (VBEMS) as a successor governing body to EMSAC, with 21 voting members (13 geographic, 8 stakeholder), standing subcommittees, an executive committee, and a self-perpetuating membership model. Discussion raised concerns about the stakeholder-nomination process, representation balance, and members receiving materials too close to the meeting. A roll call vote was conducted and the motion passed.

=== MEETING: 2026-03-18 ===
topics: [rulemaking, workforce, public-health]

Meeting held at the Waterbury State Office Complex. The Governance Workgroup reported on a proposal to transition Vermont's EMS system from a district-based structure to a unified statewide model, including elevating the EMS Office to division-level status and creating a statewide EMS Board for policy and standards while the EMS Office retains enforcement authority. The committee discussed representation, regional coordination, and division of responsibilities, finding no significant opposition to the conceptual model. The Education Workgroup reaffirmed support for the National Registry as the certification standard while discussing accreditation requirements and instructor credentialing. The Healthcare Integration Workgroup reported on a tiered interfacility transfer classification system. The committee held an in-depth discussion on the absence of a formal statewide trauma system and the need for a trauma registry, while noting funding for existing data platforms relies heavily on grants. The meeting schedule through mid-July was reviewed.

=== MEETING: 2026-03-06 ===
topics: [workforce, CME, licensing]

Two meetings were held back-to-back at the Vermont EMS Conference in Burlington. The Education Council, chaired by Pat Malone, focused on an update from the Education & Workforce Development Work Group. A central topic was whether Vermont should remain a National Registry state; the group reached consensus in favor, citing exam reliability and the resource burden of an independent testing system. The group began distinguishing "education" from "training" and discussed moving toward an organization-based (rather than individual-instructor-based) model for EMS course delivery, along with the potential role of accreditation and a possible statewide EMS academy. The Education Council meeting adjourned at 1:58 PM. The Advisory Committee meeting, chaired by Drew Hazelton, then convened at 2:00 PM; the source text is truncated shortly after roll call for this second meeting.

=== MEETING: 2026-02-18 ===
topics: [rulemaking, workforce]

Held at Middlebury Regional EMS. Minutes from the February 4 meeting were approved unanimously. The chair reported on a work group interest survey, with groups to be capped at seven members. The committee began substantive discussion on EMS system governance, identified as the foundational priority topic; staff noted 19 other states maintain statewide EMS plans, citing Maine and California as reference models. A historical overview of Vermont's district-based governance structure was presented, noting districts have statutory authority to collect funds and employ staff but rarely exercise it. The committee reviewed prior governance work, including a draft model consolidating districts into fewer regions. Unresolved challenges identified included the lack of statutory minimum EMS standards, absence of performance metrics, and limited enforcement tools. The committee agreed governance reforms should preserve hospital-based medical direction as a system strength.

=== MEETING: 2025-12-10 ===
topics: [legislation, workforce, rulemaking]

Held at the Vermont EMS Academy in Newfane. Minutes from the December 3 meeting were approved unanimously. Will Moran, state EMS Chief, welcomed new committee members and asked members to extend professionalism to Health Department staff. The committee's primary business was finalizing its statutory legislative report ahead of the December 15 deadline. A motion to table adoption of the report, citing late distribution and lack of review time for VDH legal/policy staff, was made by Chelsea Dubie and seconded by Marc Hachey; a proposed amendment to instead hold a special vote by December 15 was withdrawn, and the tabling motion itself failed by roll call, 4 yes–8 no–1 abstain. The committee recessed to review the report, then resumed detailed review covering Public Safety Telecommunications language, glossary sourcing, and air ambulance data. A motion to accept the report as amended, limiting further changes to formatting only, passed 11 yes–0 no–2 abstain. The committee also voted to ask the legislature to amend the Ambulance Provider Tax Form and to re-contract with MedOps under a $100,000 cap. Drew Hazelton was renominated as chair and Pat Malone as vice chair for 2026, both unanimous.

=== MEETING: 2025-12-03 ===
topics: [legislation, workforce, public-health]

Held at Alumni Hall in Barre. Minutes from the November 19 meeting were approved unanimously, updated to reflect Billy Fritz's attendance. The Office of EMS gave an update on the EMS Special Fund sub-recipient grant application window (January 1–31, 2026). The Education Council met for the first time in over a year and voted to endorse the Advanced EMT Student Minimum Competencies guidelines developed by State EMS Training Coordinator Courtney Newman. The committee discussed the state's Rural Health Care Transformation Grant application, noting EMS was nearly absent from it; Pat Malone suggested a letter of concern, others suggested inviting the AHS Secretary to a meeting. The committee reviewed a new Public Safety Telecommunications Task Force report on dispatch modernization (statewide CAD, interoperability standards, new oversight board) and identified gaps including no mention of priority medical dispatch. The committee then worked through its own legislative draft report section by section but did not have time to complete the full narrative review, continuing at the next meeting.

=== MEETING: 2025-11-19 ===
topics: [workforce, public-health, legislation]

Held at Williston Fire Department. Minutes from the November 5 meeting were approved with one abstention. Drew Hazelton reported committee expenditures to date of $32,172.78. The committee reviewed submitted public comments on the draft EMS system assessment, reaching consensus findings across several categories: Overall System Performance (inequitable access, inconsistent interfacility transport reliability, rising call volumes and complexity); Governance (inconsistent medical direction and governance, the district system unchanged since the 1970s); Data (inconsistent data collection, lack of standardized entry, incomplete cost and education data); Finance (outdated fee-for-service model, inadequate Medicaid reimbursement, no sustainable MIH reimbursement pathway); Quality Measures (Vermont below national benchmarks on some measures, though documentation gaps limit certainty); and System Reliability (limited reliability data beyond a Cambridge heat map). Public comments emphasized rural-versus-urban differences and equitable representation. A rough draft was slated for review at the December 3 meeting.

=== MEETING: 2025-11-05 ===
topics: [public-health, workforce, rulemaking]

Held at the Waterbury State Office Complex. Barb Neal, executive director of E911 and co-chair of the Public Safety Communications Task Force (established under Act 78 of 2023), presented on preliminary recommendations including a statewide CAD-to-CAD system, Rapid SOS platform expansion, and a proposed independent governance board; of $11 million originally appropriated, $1 million had been spent and $570,000 was authorized. The committee raised questions on EMS/ImageTrend data integration and financial impacts of CAD licensing. Minutes from October 15 were approved unanimously. The Blueprint for Health team continued discussions on interfacility transfer coordination, identifying limited bed availability and poor hospital-EMS communication as key issues. The committee reviewed the draft EMS System Report outline (ten major sections) with a feedback deadline of November 19 and a planned final vote in December. A data validation discussion addressed non-mandatory SIREN fields (e.g., lights-and-sirens use) and led to agreement on forming an EMS data technical advisory group.

=== MEETING: 2025-10-15 ===
topics: [workforce, rulemaking, public-health]

Held at Regional Ambulance in Rutland. Minutes from August 20 and September 3 were approved unanimously. Courtney Newman presented on Advanced EMT Student Minimum Competencies, noting Vermont's January 2024 adoption of competency-based evaluation in place of psychomotor exams; the item was referred to the Education Subcommittee. David Danforth summarized IFT/Blueprint for Health findings, noting fewer than half of interfacility transports meet CMS medical-necessity standards. David Danforth and Charles Piso presented the EMS Governance Subcommittee's three-region model (Northwest, Northeast, Southern) with a nine-member state EMS board and an estimated $2.6 million annual cost; a motion to circulate the proposal for feedback and revisit it in December passed with a friendly amendment. The committee reviewed statewide assessment data (~100,901 calls, 28,000 interfacility transfers, ~$100 million system cost) and NEMSQA performance metrics showing Vermont at or slightly below national averages for hypoglycemia treatment, lights-and-sirens safety compliance, stroke assessment, and trauma documentation. A motion to accept the data report as presented carried without objection.

=== MEETING: 2025-09-17 ===
topics: [workforce, legislation]

Held at the Waterbury State Office Complex. Minutes from August 20 and September 3 were approved unanimously, as was the fiscal sponsor's (Rescue Inc.) financial report. Four UVM interns were secured to support data and GIS work on the legislative report. The committee discussed the conceptual EMS governance structure from the prior meeting, raising concerns about regional equity under a three-region model, reliance on uncompensated volunteer administrators, and the need for dedicated staff positions; a Governance sub-group was formed (Leslie Lindquist, Charles Piso, David Danforth, Drew Hazelton, Dan Wolfson, Mark Hachey, Will Moran). Drew Hazelton presented preliminary fire-based EMS cohort finance data (11 licensed services, ~28,000 calls, $34.8 million spent, average cost per call $1,100), noting data limitations and consensus not to report out-of-service days by cohort. The committee agreed any report recommendation would require 75% approval of members present.

=== MEETING: 2025-09-03 ===
topics: [workforce, public-health]

Held at White River Valley Ambulance in Bethel. Drew Hazelton presented a conceptual EMS governance and organization model, describing Vermont's 13 independent EMS districts as "silos" lacking a statewide mass-casualty-incident plan and cohesive data strategy, and proposed replacing districts with a Statewide EMS Council organized into regions (e.g., Northwest, Northeast, South) under core tenets of collaboration, communication, coordination, planning, and quality. Discussion covered council size (one proposal: nine members, three per region) and legal questions about fund management. On medical direction, the committee discussed District Medical Advisors' limited (~2 hours/month) availability and options including a contractual Medical Resource Hospital model or regional DMAs. Pat Malone presented education and workforce data: Vermont exam pass rates exceeded national averages for AEMT and paramedic but licensure completion from course entry was only about 32% for EMT students, prompting discussion of instructor performance variability and higher-education partnerships with CCV and VSU.

=== MEETING: 2025-08-20 ===
topics: [workforce, CME]

Held at Chester Fire Department. The August 6 meeting minutes were approved unanimously. Pat Malone reported on securing UVM interns to assist with data synthesis. Pat Malone presented on the EMS instructor system, finding that 12 Senior Instructors (7 full-time) delivered a large majority of EMT, AEMT, and paramedic courses, while most of the 56 licensed Instructor Coordinators taught no EMT courses in 2023–2024, raising accountability concerns relative to the Fire Academy model. The committee discussed course distribution (62 EMT courses across 11 of 13 districts), the largely unknown true cost of EMS education, and comparisons to Firefighter I course costs. The Vermont Emergency First Responder (VEFR) program, created during COVID, was discussed as an effective recruitment pipeline into EMR/EMT training, with consensus that its scope should remain limited. Next steps included continued CCV collaboration discussions and district-level cost data collection.

=== MEETING: 2025-08-06 ===
topics: [workforce, public-health, rulemaking]

Held at the Waterbury State Office Complex. The July 9 and July 16 minutes were approved with one abstention. The committee adopted a working definition of Emergency Medical Services for use in the forthcoming five-year plan; a friendly amendment inserted "appropriate" before "disposition" to address legal/ethical concerns about EMS determining patient outcomes, and the amended definition passed unanimously by roll call. EMS Data Manager Connor Dunn presented proposed performance metrics drawn from NEMSQA/NEMSIS/CARES standards; the committee voted unanimously to adopt four measures (Hypoglycemia 01, Trauma 08, Safety 02, Stroke 01) and separately voted to authorize the fiscal agent to pay reasonable NEMSIS data-retrieval fees. The committee reviewed service inventory progress (financial-data reporting response rate up from 20% to 70%) and interfacility transport data cleanup, including removal of over 2,000 out-of-state records. Staffing capacity, documentation quality, and the December 2025 legislative deadline were flagged as key challenges.

=== MEETING: 2025-07-16 ===
topics: [workforce, public-health]

Held at Northeastern Vermont Regional Hospital. William Moran presented a financial update: of a $370,000 Act 157 allocation, $148,200 had been spent, leaving $221,800, plus a separate $40,000 annual operating allocation. The committee noted that no official minutes existed for the July 9 meeting due to a failure of an AI-based note-taking system, requiring manual reconstruction. The primary discussion concerned performance measures for the EMS Five-Year Strategic Plan, focusing on adopting standardized NEMSQA/NEMSIS clinical metrics rather than custom measures, and operational/system-reliability measures such as ambulance coverage gaps and mutual-aid tracking. Dr. Daniel Wolfson reported on Vermont's CARES cardiac arrest registry program and a new collaboration with the 911 Board on time-to-dispatch data. The committee discussed progress toward procuring the Biospatial data platform and agreed to form technical advisory groups (TAGs) covering clinical, operational, education/workforce, and financial domains.

=== MEETING: 2025-07-09 ===
topics: [workforce, legislation]

A retreat-style meeting held at the Department of Health, Waterbury State Office Complex, facilitated by Katherine Sims. Participants opened by sharing individual goals for the day. The committee refined its draft EMS definition, vision, and guiding principles, producing a longer working definition describing EMS as "an integrated practice of medicine" spanning public safety, public health, healthcare, and emergency management. A draft six-month timeline was set, running from a July 16 EMSAC meeting on definitions and benchmarks through an October target to select a contractor for the five-year plan, with two rounds of planned public messaging. The committee mapped governance roles (local/regional/state) across functions including MCI planning, education, interfacility transport, licensing, clinical standards, dispatch, and mobile integrated health, and drafted an outline for the eventual EMS Final Report. Next steps included validating service-level data with districts and a public "roadshow" work group. The meeting closed with individual reflections from participants.
