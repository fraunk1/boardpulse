# Michigan Board of Osteopathic Medicine and Surgery — 12-Month Board Summary

**Period:** 2025-08-07 to 2026-06-04
**Meetings analyzed:** 6

Over the past year, the Michigan Board of Osteopathic Medicine and Surgery and its Disciplinary Subcommittee (DSC) met on a bimonthly cycle, though two full board sessions were cancelled: October 2, 2025 ([2025-10-02](/board/MI/MI_DO#2025-10-02)) and June 4, 2026, when both the full board and DSC meetings were cancelled ([2026-06-04](/board/MI/MI_DO#2026-06-04)). Board activity centered on individual licensee discipline and reinstatement. At the August 7, 2025 meeting, the full board denied reinstatement to one licensee and granted reinstatement with one year of probation to another, while the DSC suspended a physician's license for one year with a $5,000 fine and accepted consent orders, including one rejected with a counteroffer tied to continuing-education completion ([2025-08-07](/board/MI/MI_DO#2025-08-07)). On October 2, 2025, the DSC dissolved a summary suspension, fined and revoked a respondent's license, accepted a previously tabled consent order, and dismissed a case ([2025-10-02](/board/MI/MI_DO#2025-10-02)). At the December 4, 2025 meetings, the full board granted one reinstatement with one year of probation and a second with a five-year limitation prohibiting billable telehealth visits, while the DSC accepted several consent orders, rejected one with a one-year-probation counteroffer, and dismissed a case ([2025-12-04](/board/MI/MI_DO#2025-12-04)). On February 5, 2026, the full board granted reinstatement with a five-year limitation prohibiting controlled-substance activity and Michigan Medical Marihuana Program certifications, and re-elected Stephen Bell as chairperson and Khawaja Ikram as vice-chairperson; the DSC accepted four consent orders and rejected one with a counteroffer limiting the respondent's controlled-substances prescribing for two years alongside continuing education and chart-review requirements ([2026-02-05](/board/MI/MI_DO#2026-02-05)). The April 2, 2026 agendas listed five DSC consent-order matters, one administrative-complaint proposal for decision, and a continuing-education waiver request for the full board, but approved minutes documenting outcomes were not available ([2026-04-02](/board/MI/MI_DO#2026-04-02)).

Recurring themes across the year included consent order and stipulation review, petitions and proposals for reinstatement (several tied to controlled-substances or telehealth practice limitations), Health Professional Recovery Program (HPRP) oversight and a vacant HPRC representative seat, and routine board governance such as committee assignments and conflict-of-interest disclosures ([2025-08-07](/board/MI/MI_DO#2025-08-07), [2025-12-04](/board/MI/MI_DO#2025-12-04), [2026-02-05](/board/MI/MI_DO#2026-02-05)). No discussion of AI, general telehealth policy, IMLC, or pending legislation appeared in the available materials.

=== MEETING: 2026-02-05 ===
topics: ["disciplinary", "controlled-substances", "licensing"]

The Disciplinary Subcommittee, chaired by Ayanna Neal, approved a master resolution and acted on five consent order matters: it accepted stipulations for Jason Edward Brunt, DO; Charles Mok, DO; William John Powers, DO; and Craig Ivan Schwartz, DO; and rejected the stipulation for Annemarie Poleck, DO with a counteroffer imposing a two-year license limitation barring controlled-substance prescribing, 15 hours of addiction-medicine continuing education, quarterly chart reviews, and a $4,000 fine. The DSC also accepted an order of dismissal for Gerard Joseph Mahoney, DO. Separately, the full board, chaired by Stephen Bell, granted reinstatement to John Patrick Swan, DO with a five-year limitation prohibiting controlled-substance activity and Michigan Medical Marihuana Program certifications, re-elected Bell as chairperson and Khawaja Ikram as vice-chairperson, made committee assignments, reviewed the HPRP annual report, and reviewed the master resolution with no changes.
